What Is 2 Point Perspective?

2 point perspective is a drawing method that uses two vanishing points placed on the horizon line. It is commonly used to draw buildings, boxes, city scenes, and complex architectural structures.

Unlike 1 point perspective, where depth converges to a single point, 2 point perspective creates more realistic angular views by directing lines toward two separate vanishing points.

How 2 Point Perspective Works

In this method:

  • Vertical lines remain vertical
  • Horizontal edges converge toward either vanishing point
  • No lines remain fully horizontal except the horizon

This allows artists to create dynamic corner views of structures instead of flat frontal views.

Common Mistakes in 2 Point Perspective

Many beginners accidentally keep horizontal lines parallel. In 2 point perspective, all horizontal edges must converge toward one of the two vanishing points. A structured grid helps prevent this mistake.
